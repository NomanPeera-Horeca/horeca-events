The Horeca Meetup is built around serious owners and operators. VIP is not a upsell — it is how we mark guests who should get unmistakable hospitality, a clear welcome at the door, and zero ambiguity that their evening matters to us.

Why we call someone VIP

So the right hosts feel it before they walk in.

We use VIP for owners, multi-unit leaders, and voices we want amplified in the room — people we may invite personally or approve with intent. The goal is simple: when they arrive, everything should signal we expected you, not you are one more name on a list.

01

Priority welcome & check-in

VIPs receive a dedicated confirmation and pass treatment at entry — staff know to expect them. No hunting for a name; the evening starts with respect.

02

VIP confirmation email & digital entry pass

Approved VIPs get a separate email from standard guests: distinct design, “VIP Host” framing, and the same practical tools (calendar links and personal QR) so nothing is lost in logistics.

03

Room placement & hospitality notes

We hold space with intent — closer to the conversation, not the fringe. If they share dietary needs, accessibility, or a quiet word with our team before doors open, we route it to the people who can act on it.

04

Direct line to the events team

VIPs can reply to their confirmation or write events@thehorecastore.com. That thread is the fastest path for real questions — not a form buried on a website.

05

Recognition among peers (without theater)

We do not run a red carpet. We do run a room where the right people know they were chosen for a reason. That social proof matters when you are trying to pull serious operators into one conversation.

VIP is extended by invitation or by approval. If you believe you should be in this tier for an upcoming edition, say so and we will consider it — we cap the room intentionally so every VIP seat still means something.

For your ops team

How VIP ties to email & check-in

In production, VIP is a flag on the guest record. When you approve someone marked VIP, the system sends the VIP approval template (not the standard confirmation). At the door, VIP appears on the roster so staff greet them correctly.

  1. Source files (repo): emails/approval-vip.html — also mirrored at project root as approval-vip.html.
  2. Database: template slug approval-vip (category approval), applied via database/upsert_email_template_approval_vip.sql if you have not run it yet.
  3. Admin: mark the guest as VIP before (or after) approval; the approve flow pre-selects the VIP template when the flag is on.
