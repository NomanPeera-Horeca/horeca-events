Invitation tier

A seat reserved for the people we want
at the center of the room.

The Horeca Meetup is built around serious owners and operators. VIP is not an upsell. It is how we mark guests who should get unmistakable hospitality, a clear welcome at the door, a host shoutout, plated dinner, and zero ambiguity that their evening matters to us.

Why we call someone VIP

So the right hosts feel it before they walk in.

We use VIP for owners, multi-unit leaders, and voices we want amplified in the room: people we may invite personally or approve with intent. The goal is simple: when they arrive, everything should signal we expected you, not you are one more name on a list.

01

Priority welcome & check-in

VIPs receive a dedicated confirmation and pass treatment at entry. Staff know to expect them. No hunting for a name; the evening starts with respect.

02

VIP confirmation email & digital entry pass

Approved VIPs get a separate email from standard guests: distinct design, VIP Host framing, and the same practical tools (calendar links and personal QR) so logistics stay simple.

03

VIP shoutout by the host

During the program, the host acknowledges our VIP guests by name. It is a public thank-you, not a speech: you are visible to the room as someone we wanted there.

04

Plated dinner

VIP guests are served a plated dinner at the table. You stay in the conversation instead of the buffet line. The standard guest experience may remain reception-style or buffet; VIP is seated service for the meal.

05

Room placement & hospitality notes

We hold space with intent: closer to the conversation, not the fringe. If you share dietary needs, accessibility needs, or timing constraints before doors open, we route them to the team that can act on them.

06

Direct line for questions & special requests

For anything that needs a decision, not a ticket: reach our CEO, noman.peera@thehorecastore.com. That includes unusual scheduling, accessibility, dietary restrictions we should coordinate in advance, or a referral you want handled personally. General RSVP and event logistics can also go to events@thehorecastore.com.

07

Recognition among peers (without theater)

We do not run a red carpet. We do run a room where the right people know they were chosen for a reason. That social proof matters when you are trying to pull serious operators into one conversation.

VIP is extended by invitation or by approval. We cap the room intentionally so every VIP seat still means something. Tell us you are interested through the application, or write the CEO if your situation needs a conversation first.
