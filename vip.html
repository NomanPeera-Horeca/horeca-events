What this is

Who Horeca Store is, and why we run this room.

Horeca Store is the global hospitality supply company behind this series. We equip hotels, restaurants, cafes, and franchise operations with furniture, equipment, smallwares, and operating supplies across the Middle East, Asia, and the United States. The properties and brands that rely on us number 15,000+ worldwide. The meetup is not a catalogue pitch. It is our way of gathering serious operators in one place. Learn more at thehorecastore.com

Why we organize The Horeca Meetup: We host a private, quarterly dinner for owners and leaders in markets where we work and where our guests work. The room is curated and invitation-led so the conversation stays high-trust: peers talking shop with peers, not a noisy expo hall. We cap attendance on purpose. If you are in the room, it is because we believe you belong in that circle.

Why VIP exists: Most approved guests receive a strong standard experience. VIP is the tier we use when someone should feel unmistakably expected: a multi-unit leader, a voice we want amplified in the program, a personal invite, or a referral we intend to honor. If that sounds like you, use Apply on this page: it opens the same form as the main site, with your row flagged VIP for our team. The numbered list below is what we deliver when we mark someone VIP.

Why we call someone VIP

So the right hosts feel it before they walk in.

We use VIP for owners, multi-unit leaders, and voices we want amplified in the room: people we may invite personally or approve with intent. The goal is simple: when they arrive, everything should signal we expected you, not you are one more name on a list.

01

Priority welcome & check-in

VIPs receive a dedicated confirmation and pass treatment at entry. Staff know to expect them. No hunting for a name; the evening starts with respect.

02

VIP confirmation email & digital entry pass

Approved VIPs get a separate email from standard guests: distinct design, VIP Host framing, and the same practical tools (calendar links and personal QR) so logistics stay simple.

03

VIP shoutout by the host

During the program, the host acknowledges our VIP guests by name. It is a public thank-you, not a speech: you are visible to the room as someone we wanted there.

04

Plated dinner

VIP guests are served a plated dinner at the table. You stay in the conversation instead of the buffet line. The standard guest experience may remain reception-style or buffet; VIP is seated service for the meal.

05

Room placement & hospitality notes

We hold space with intent: closer to the conversation, not the fringe. If you share dietary needs, accessibility needs, or timing constraints before doors open, we route them to the team that can act on them.

06

Direct line for questions & special requests

For anything that needs a decision, not a ticket: noman.peera@thehorecastore.com. That includes unusual scheduling, accessibility, dietary restrictions we should coordinate in advance, or a referral you want handled personally. General RSVP and event logistics: events@thehorecastore.com.

07

Recognition among peers (without theater)

We do not run a red carpet. We do run a room where the right people know they were chosen for a reason. That social proof matters when you are trying to pull serious operators into one conversation.

VIP is extended by invitation or by approval. We cap the room intentionally so every VIP seat still means something. Use the application link: your registration arrives with the VIP flag for our team. If your situation needs a personal thread first, use the direct line in item 06.
