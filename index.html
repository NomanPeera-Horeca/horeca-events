01 · Why Attend

Six reasons to give up one Tuesday night.

i.

Find your next franchise partner.

The room is built for operators ready to scale. Half of last edition's attendees walked out with a serious franchise conversation already started. Multi-unit operators looking for territory partners. Single-unit owners hunting for proven concepts. The match-making happens over dinner.

ii.

Make your restaurant more profitable.

Honest conversations about real margins. What is working in 2026, what is not, and the unsexy decisions that quietly add 4 to 8 points to your bottom line. Operators sharing actual P&L numbers under the Chatham House Rule. No theory. No consultants. Just the math that matters.

iii.

The network that actually scales businesses.

One hundred of Houston's most ambitious restaurant and hotel owners under one roof. This is the room where suppliers get found, partners get hired, leases get sublet, and operators help operators solve problems no LinkedIn post ever could. The relationships compound for years.

iv.

Grow your operation, without growing chaos.

Going from 1 to 3 locations is a different game than going from 3 to 10. The room is filled with operators who have crossed those bridges and survived. Hire systems, supplier relationships, financing playbooks, and the staffing strategies that keep multi-unit operators sane.

v.

A private dinner, done right.

Welcome reception at 6:00 PM. A generous buffet dinner served at 8:30 PM. Curated room flow arranged by operator profile, not by surname. The Marriott Energy Corridor handles hospitality the way operators expect it done. The dinner alone makes the night worth the drive.

vi.

You become part of the series.

Vol. II is the second Houston edition. Vol. III opens September. Vol. IV in January. Vol. V in May. Once you are in the room, you are in the room. The series compounds. The network multiplies. The earlier you join, the deeper the relationships build over time.

02 · Manifesto

Why this room exists.

Running a restaurant in 2026 is harder than it has ever been. Margins are tighter. Staff is harder to keep. The noise from "restaurant gurus" online has never been louder, or less useful.

So we built a different room.

The Horeca Meetup is a quarterly private dinner for the operators who are actually doing the work. Building, scaling, and surviving in this market. Not the people selling them tools. Not the people selling them advice. The ones in the kitchen at 6am and the dining room at midnight.
